Microsoft's GB300 AI Factory Goes Live

Microsoft flipped the switch on Azure's first GB300 NVL72 cluster—4,600+ GPUs networked as a single accelerator. Satya Nadella calls it the "first of many." Bottom line: Microsoft can run frontier reasoning models at scale today, not next year. This is the new baseline for hyperscale AI infrastructure.

THIS WEEK'S SIGNAL

OpenAI Goes Custom Silicon

OpenAI partnered with Broadcom to build custom AI chips targeting 10 GW of compute by 2029. The signal: diversify beyond Nvidia/AMD to control per-token economics.

Reuters ↗

AMD Lands OpenAI—Plus Equity

OpenAI signed a multi-year GPU supply deal with AMD and granted a warrant for up to 160M shares (performance-contingent). Translation: real volumes, material alignment, multi-vendor forever.

Sora 2 Explodes Out of the Gate

OpenAI's Sora 2 video app hit 1M+ downloads in <5 days. The creator-rights debate is heating up fast. Short-form, AI-native video just went mainstream.

Google Swings at Enterprise

Google launched Gemini Enterprise (chat-with-your-work-data) and embedded Gemini in Chrome for on-page help. Clear shot at Microsoft's Copilot dominance.

Multi-Model Becomes the Norm
